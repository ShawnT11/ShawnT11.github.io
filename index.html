Featured explainer

What Is Generative AI?

Generative AI is software that creates new content from instructions. It can draft text, summarize documents, write code, generate images, and help users explore ideas faster.

The short answer

Instead of only sorting or predicting information, generative AI produces something new based on patterns it learned during training. The output can be useful, but it still needs human review.

Why it matters

For everyday users, generative AI can act like a writing assistant, study partner, brainstorming tool, or coding helper. The real value comes from asking clear questions and checking the result before using it.

What to watch out for

AI tools can make mistakes, invent sources, repeat bias, or expose private information if users paste sensitive data. Good AI literacy means knowing when the tool is useful and when a trusted source is required.

Safe AI checklist

Use AI faster without handing it your judgment.

Do not paste sensitive information, do not trust unsourced claims, and do not let a polished answer replace your own review. The strongest AI habit is simple: ask clearly, verify carefully, and edit before using.
