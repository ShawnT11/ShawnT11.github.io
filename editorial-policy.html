Editorial Policy

How The AI Explainer publishes AI guides

The AI Explainer is written for readers who want useful AI literacy without hype. The site favors clear explanations, practical examples, and safety notes over short keyword summaries.

Originality

Articles are written for this site and organized around reader questions. The site does not republish copied articles, scraped pages, or copyrighted reports. When external documentation, research, or product information is used, articles should explain the point in original wording and link to the relevant source when helpful.

Review and updates

Articles are reviewed for plain language, practical usefulness, and risk warnings. AI changes quickly, so pages may be updated when a guide needs clearer examples, better navigation, or improved safety context.

Article updates may include a revised review date, additional examples, source links, clearer warnings, or new related resources. Changes are made to improve reader value rather than to repeat keywords.

Source use

Source links are used when they help readers verify concepts, safety practices, or policy context. The site favors primary or established sources such as standards bodies, public agencies, official documentation, and reputable educational material. Source links are not paid placements.

AI assistance disclosure

AI tools may be used to help draft outlines, generate wording alternatives, or check readability. Final pages should be reviewed for usefulness, originality, accuracy signals, and safety context before publication. The site does not intentionally publish copied articles, scraped pages, or unreviewed AI output.

Responsible AI coverage

The site avoids presenting AI tools as perfect or fully autonomous. Guides explain what AI can help with, what people should verify, and where human judgment remains necessary.

Corrections

If a page contains a factual mistake, unclear wording, broken source link, or copyright concern, the contact page explains the correction scope. Personal AI consulting and private troubleshooting are outside the site's publishing purpose.

Advertising boundary

Advertising must not replace the article experience. Ads, when present, should not imitate navigation, mislead readers, interrupt essential content, or encourage accidental clicks.
